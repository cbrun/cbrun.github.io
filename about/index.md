## Turning languages into practices

I joined [Obeo](https://www.obeosoft.com/en/) as an intern in 2006, after discovering modeling and model-driven engineering through university courses in Nantes. What began as a technical interest in metamodels, generators, and graphical editors became a much broader question: **how do we make formal languages genuinely useful to the people and organizations that depend on them?**

Today, as CEO of Obeo, I help shape the technologies, products, partnerships, and open-source ecosystems behind that question. My perspective remains grounded in building software: understanding the abstractions, testing them against real engineering work, and learning where technology alone is not enough.

<p class="about-statement">A language is not finished when it is defined. It becomes valuable when people can practice it, evolve it, and trust the ecosystem around it.</p>

## A path through modeling

<div class="about-timeline">
  <article>
    <p class="about-timeline__marker">2006</p>
    <div>
      <h3>Starting with languages and generators</h3>
      <p>I had already started contributing to open-source projects including KDE and the Linux kernel when I joined Obeo at the beginning of its story. Work around <a href="https://eclipse.dev/acceleo/">Acceleo</a>, code generation, and model-driven engineering showed me how open technology could give a small team reach, credibility, and a durable relationship with its users.</p>
    </div>
  </article>
  <article>
    <p class="about-timeline__marker">Eclipse</p>
    <div>
      <h3>From individual tools to shared platforms</h3>
      <p>My work expanded across projects including <a href="https://eclipse.dev/emf/compare/">EMF Compare</a>, EcoreTools, and <a href="https://eclipse.dev/sirius/">Eclipse Sirius</a>. The recurring lesson was that a useful tool needs more than a good technical core: it needs a community, governance, documentation, and room for others to build on it.</p>
    </div>
  </article>
  <article>
    <p class="about-timeline__marker">Today</p>
    <div>
      <h3>Building the next generation openly</h3>
      <p>My current focus includes the transition to web-based modeling, the emergence of SysML v2, <a href="https://mbse-syson.org/">Eclipse SysON</a>, and the continued evolution of the <a href="https://mbse-capella.org/">Capella and Arcadia ecosystem</a>. The goal is not simply to ship more tools, but to create foundations that organizations can understand, influence, and sustain.</p>
    </div>
  </article>
</div>
