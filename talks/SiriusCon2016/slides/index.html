The Foundations of Sirius

Achieved through fundamentals, let's call them the pilars of Sirius.
I numbered 4 of them.

Real-World ready

We tend to present simplistic examples, but since its inception tailored for complex domains. A complex domain can easily grow in the 100s of inter-related concepts. Wiring of an A350.

“Do not compromise on your domain model.
Tooling and metamodels are orthogonal.”

We learned that never having to compromise, change your domain model, for the tools, is one of the key factor of success
First: total flexibility in regard to your metamodel.
In the real world these things happens. You better be prepared for it instead of hoping.

OpenSource

Play well with other technologies

OSS, I'll get back on that later on, but more important that it fits an environment.
A pretty classic vision of your tool. Metamodel at the middle (do not compromise!). Several technologies involved already.
All of those are integrated into a common platform, Eclipse, which gives UI consistency (and software updates, and multi-platform UI...)
And this is it. Your product is actually an aggregate. That's why working seamlessly with other technologies is key!
Interesting in the way we build Sirius. The way you do things is often the key factor in success. In our case, 100% open and transparent.

These are the pillars we build the technology on, and the OSS/Transparency is the way we do things. You can watch, and you can even participate. That's what started to happened in 2016: people getting involved.
